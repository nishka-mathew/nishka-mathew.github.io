About

I graduated with a degree in Computer Science and Psychology from UNC Charlotte with a 3.83 GPA. My interests sit at the intersection of behavioral science, UX research, and machine learning — because I believe the most effective systems are built on a thorough understanding of human behavior.

"Most people are researchers or developers. I graduated learning why you need both — you have to understand people well enough to build systems that actually work for them."

My research foundation began in my psychology courses and expanded when I became a Teaching Assistant for Research Methodology II, guiding students through the full research pipeline. I then applied that foundation as a Research Intern in the SPEX Lab, co-authoring a peer-reviewed paper published at ACM COMPASS 2025.

On the technical side, my machine learning work moved quickly from coursework into a real-world setting — building predictive models for microalgae biomass growth using live sensor data, then designing interactive visualizations to make those predictions accessible to non-technical stakeholders.

When I'm not in a research or designing mode, I'm convincing my two cats that my laptop is not a bed, or looking into what it actually takes to get a recreational pilot's license.
