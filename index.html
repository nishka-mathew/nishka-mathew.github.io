About

I graduated with a degree in Computer Science and Psychology from UNC Charlotte with a 3.83 GPA. My interests sit at the intersection of behavioral science, UX research, and machine learning because I believe the most effective data systems are built on a thorough understanding of human behavior.

My research foundation began in my psychology courses and expanded when I became a Teaching Assistant for Research Methodology II, guiding students through the full research pipeline. I then applied that foundation in the Security, Privacy, and User Experience (SPEX) Lab, supporting a PhD student starting from a literature review to publishing a peer-reviewed research paper in ACM COMPASS 2025.

On the technical side, my machine learning and data analytics work began in my Data Mining course and moved quickly into a real-world setting. Developing predictive models for microalgae biomass growth using live sensor data, and building interactive visualizations to make those predictions accessible to non-technical stakeholders.

Most people are researchers or developers. I graduated learning why you need both. You have to understand people well enough to build systems that actually work for them.

AI Microalgae Biomass Predictor
Built a predictive ML model to forecast microalgae biomass growth for EcoClosure's Biochromic Window systems, a living building technology that uses microalgae for energy regulation and carbon capture. Tested linear, logarithmic, and random forest regression across multiple preprocessing strategies first using Kaggle data, then using real sensor data from Charlotte's Innovation Barn, with min-max scaled linear regression achieving the best performance at R² = 0.911. Designed interactive visualizations including a 14-day biomass forecast and feature importance charts to make model outputs interpretable to non-technical stakeholders.
